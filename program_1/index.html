AEMON-J workshop Program

July 13 Day 1: Remote Sensing
Introductory Speaker: Nuria Sanchez Lopez (University of Idaho)
Keynote Speakers: Simon Topp (@simonNtopp; University of North Carolina
Xiao Yang (@XiaoYan20198978; University of North Carolina)
Workshop Organizers: Simon Topp (@simonNtopp; University of North Carolina)
Xiao Yang (@XiaoYan20198978; University of North Carolina)
New developments in remote sensing data collection have led to a myriad of new research possibilites for terrestrial and aquatic research alike. Some of the most striking advantages of remote sensing data over sensor data are the high horizontal spatial dimension of the collected data and the possibility to collect data from regions that are difficult to access. This day, we will learn what can be done with this type of data and how to download and use it.

July 14 Day 2: Big (Ecological) Data
Introductory Speaker: Kaelin Cawley (@KaelinCawley; National Ecological Observatory Network)
Keynote Speaker: Ryan McClure (@ryan_mclake; Virgina Polytechnical Institute and State University)
Workshop Organizer: Kaelin Cawley (@KaelinCawley; National Ecological Observatory Network)
Large datasets are becoming more and more prominent in aquatic research, as our ability to collect data at high spatial and temporal resolution increases, and thanks to efforts to construct long ecological time series. However, the opportunities that such datasets offer, are accompanied by challenges on how to store, download, and work with such large amounts of data. The National Science Foundation's National Ecological Observatory Network (NEON) is an expert in monitoring freshwater ecosystems in the United States, and on this day, NEON employees will teach us how to work with NEON datasets.
Please see our workshop page here
July 15 Day 3: Machine Learning
Keynote Speaker: Elena Matta (Politecnico Milano)
Workshop Organizer: Mridul Thomas (University of Geneva)
Machine Learning is a fast-developing field for discovering patterns and correlations in complex datasets. By not imposing a data structure on your data beforehand, Machine Learning can offer insights that statistical methods cannot offer. As it is a rapidly developing and relatively recent field, Machine Learning methods are still unknown to many of us, and today we will learn how Machine Learning can be applied to ecological data.
Please see our workshop page here
July 16 Day 4: Numerical Modeling
Introductory Speaker: Thomas Petzoldt (Technische Universität Dresden)
Keynote Speaker: Deviyani Gurung (Washington State University)
Workshop Organizer: Thomas Petzoldt (Technische Universität Dresden)
Numerical modeling is an established field within the aquatic sciences, and knows a wide range of application. Using models alongside observational or experimental data can lead to novel understanding, and by constructing a model, potential scenarios can be assessed, for example in a forecasting framework. On this last day of the workshop, we will learn more about the possibilities of numerical modelling and how to construct our own model in R.
