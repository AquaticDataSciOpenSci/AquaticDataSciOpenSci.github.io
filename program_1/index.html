AEMON-J workshop Program

July 25 Day 1: Remote Sensing
Introductory Speakers: Nuria Sanchez Lopez (@Sanchez_LopezN; University of Idaho) & Tyler King (U.S. Geological Survey)
Keynote Speaker: Kate Fickas-Naleway (@katefickas; U.S. Geological Survey)
Workshop Organizers: Victoria Stengel (@VictoriaStengel; U.S. Geological Survey) & Tyler King (U.S. Geological Survey)
New developments in remote sensing data collection have led to a myriad of new research possibilites for terrestrial and aquatic research alike. Some of the most striking advantages of remote sensing data over sensor data are the high horizontal spatial dimension of the collected data and the possibility to collect data from regions that are difficult to access. This day, we will learn what can be done with this type of data and how to download and use it.

July 27 Day 3: Numerical Modeling
Introductory Speakers: Nicolas Azaña Schnedler-Meyer (Technical University of Denmark) & Tobias Kuhlmann Andersen (@TobiasKAndersen; Aarhus University)
Keynote Speaker: Dennis Trolle (@DennisTrolle; Aarhus University)
Workshop Organizers: Tobias Kuhlmann Andersen (@TobiasKAndersen; Aarhus University) & Nicolas Azaña Schnedler-Meyer (Technical University of Denmark)
The development and application of numerical aquatic ecosystem models has been a rapidly growing field in aquatic sciences. In an effort to understand the functioning of aquatic ecosystems and to aid in restoring and preserving water bodies, for instance by scenario analysis or in a forecasting framework, aquatic ecosystem models has emerged as powerful tools for science and management. In this workshop, we will learn more about the possibilities of numerical aquatic ecosystem models and how to configure, execute and apply the Water Ecosystems Tool model.
