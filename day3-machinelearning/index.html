Day 3: Machine Learning Thursday 15 July 2021 15:00-19:00 UTC
When you’re likely to gain from machine learning, and when not: A mildly sceptical introduction for beginners
Mridul K. Thomas (@mridulkthomas; University of Geneva)
Machine learning has tremendous, revolutionary possibilities. But as a beginner at data analysis, it’s hard to know when it’s worth reaching beyond your basic statistical toolbox into the exploding bag of machine learning tricks. This talk (and workshop) is aimed at people who want to know in what situations the jump to basic machine learning techniques is likely to be worth it - and also when you’ve likely to get more out of traditional statistical techniques. We will talk about the simplest regression applications for which machine learning might provide an advantage. This is aimed at the beginner level and so the arguments here may not apply to more sophisticated machine learning methods.
15:00-15:45 UTC Keynote: Machine learning applications in water hydrology and hydraulics
Elena Matta (@ElenaMatta3; Politecnico di Milano - Environmental Intelligence Lab)
Machine learning (ML) represents an attractive alternative to physically based hydro-numerical models, mainly due to their cheaper computational costs, high performance, and to their lower requirement of physical and topographical data. Besides, in comparison with the traditional statistical models, stochastic methods, and empirical formulations, such techniques have a larger potential in providing accurate and reliable predictions (Zounemat-Kermani et al., 2020), fostered by the growing availability of sensor data and current straightforward open-source technology available.

This keynote speech presents an overview of some recent ML applications in water hydrology and hydraulics. Works dealing with the prediction of water level and rainfall-runoff, flood and risk assessment, sediment transport, odours in sewage systems, and water demands in the urban surface waters are shown and briefly discussed.
The keynote will be pre-recorded and available for viewing in advance.
A live stream of the pre-recorded keynote will begin at 15:00 UTC, followed immediately by a live Question and Answer session with Elena.

16:00-18:00 UTC Workshop: A brief hands-on introduction to machine learning for regression
Mridul K. Thomas (@mridulkthomas; University of Geneva)
This 90-minute workshop is intended to communicate 4 things: (1) the basic conceptual differences between machine learning and (most) traditional statistics, (2) how to fit and visualise a few basic machine learning models in R, (3) how to evaluate and interpret your machine learning model, and (4) the common failure modes and how to avoid them. The focus will be on random forests because of their relative simplicity and robustness, but we will touch on other methods as well. You will need a basic familiarity with regressions and with R (we will use RStudio) to make use of this workshop, but nothing else. This is aimed at the beginner level and so the arguments and general guidance may not always apply to more sophisticated machine learning methods.
This workshop will be live.
